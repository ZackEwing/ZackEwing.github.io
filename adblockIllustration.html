Project Scope

With over 65 million users, AdBlock is the most popular ad blocking extension on many of the most used web browsers. The company maintains a blog and help desk to engage with users via new feature announcements, how-to guides, and general updates. The team uses hero images at the top of articles and blog posts to lend context to communications, reinforce brand identity, and inspire delight.

Contribution

As lead product designer at AdBlock, I architected a new style guide and brand identity that began making its way into our products in mid-2020. In order to reinforce our new brand identity and visual language, I was tasked with delivering blog post imagery to our content marketing team for use in our user facing communications. Because AdBlock operates with a very small design team, the process we used was to first find licensed vector art that was close to our flat isometric style, then refactor and simply that art, adding new assets as needed to build images with complete narratives.
